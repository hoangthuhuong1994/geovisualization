Introduction to Geovisualization

What is Geovisualization

According to Mike J. Smith , Geovisualization deals with the development of theories, methods and tools for the visual exploration, analysis, synthesis and presentation of spatial data for specific applications and users. Visualization is the convergence of computer graphics, image processing, computer vision, computer-aided design, signal processing, and user interface studies.

Visualization is the convergence of computer graphics, image processing, computer vision, computer-aided design, signal processing, and user interface studies (McCormick, B.H., DeFanti, T.A., and Brown, M.D. (Eds.). 1987) .

Scientific visualization tools support knowledge creation and hypothesis generation (MacEachren, A.M. and Kraak, M.J. 1997) .

Purposes of Geovisualization

Geovisualization is crucial as it enhances data understanding by visually representing complex spatial information, aids decision-making processes by enabling interactive exploration and analysis, improves communication of spatial data to diverse audiences, drives innovation in various fields, facilitates exploration of multidimensional data, and enhances overall efficiency in tasks related to spatial data analysis and decision-making.

Challenges in the field of Geovisualization

  • Big Data: High dens measurements from space over long periods of time.
  • Complexity and Heterogeneity: Integration of data from different sources, multidimensional data.
  • Dynamics: Analysis and exploration of spatiotemporal changes.
  • Usability and ease of use: Users are not Programmers
  • New hardware and technologies: from Desktop to mobile, from Screen to Virtual Worlds.
